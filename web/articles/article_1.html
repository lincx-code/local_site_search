Dragon Quest XI: Echoes of an Elusive Ages

Sometimes a game doesn’t have to be particularly innovative to be good. Dragon Quest XI doesn’t have any new tricks up its sleeve. Instead, it refines the JRPG formula to its best components and pulls them off wonderfully. The turn-based combat and mysterious hero story feel delightfully retro, but there’s enough modern convenience packed in to make it still feel fresh. In a time when long-running franchises are trying (sometimes too hard) to stay relevant, Dragon Quest XI is fine with being reliable and well-built, which ironically makes it unique among its peers.
